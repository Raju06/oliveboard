Anne Smith, Violent Theft > highway robbery, 13th January 1716.

Anne Smith of the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields , was indicted for assaulting and robbing, (with one Jane Evans ,) one Richard a Negro, on the Highway, and taking from him a Silver Collar, Value 15 s. the Goods of William Jordan , on the 6th of December last. Mr. Jordan swore, he sent his Boy of an Errand, who was met by the Prisoner: and complaining to her, that he had been long on his Errand, and fear'd to go home, she told him, his Collar would betray him, and therefore advis'd him to let her take it off; and carrying him to a Fruit-Cellar, the Prisoner and Jane Evans broke the Collar with their Teeth, and took it away. After this the Boy came home, and told his Master of it, who took up the Prisoner, and carrying her before a Justice she confess'd, that Jane Evans took off the Collar, and that she sold it. The Jury considering the Matter, found her Guilty of Felony only .
